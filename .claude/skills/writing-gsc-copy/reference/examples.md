# Before / after examples

The highest-signal reference. Each pair shows the same intent, off-brand then
on-brand.

## Contents
- Hero headlines
- Body / analysis
- CTA
- Error
- Email subject

## Hero headlines

**Before:** "Welcome to a World-Class Football Investment Opportunity!"
**After:** "A crown no one else wears."
*Why:* claim not greeting; specific not hype; no exclamation.

**Before:** "Discover the exciting potential of Central American football."
**After:** "Own the game."
*Why:* three words, a verb, an accent word. Confidence over adjectives.

## Body / analysis

**Before:** "This is an incredibly undervalued asset with huge upside and a
massive, world-class fanbase that presents unparalleled opportunities."
**After:** "Priced at 1.4× revenue — below the 2.7× league median — with three
million diaspora fans already in the United States."
*Why:* every claim is a number; benchmarked; no intensifiers.

## CTA

**Before:** "Interested? Click here to learn more and get started today!"
**After:** "Request the data room."
*Why:* one action, named. Urgency isn't manufactured with punctuation.

## Error

**Before:** "Error: Your submission could not be processed (code 422)."
**After:** "That didn't go through. Check your email address and try again."
*Why:* plain, no code, tells the user the next step.

## Email subject

**Before:** "🔥 An opportunity you can't afford to miss!!!"
**After:** "Comunicaciones FC — confidential investment memo"
*Why:* specific, sober, respects the reader; no emoji in investor mail.
